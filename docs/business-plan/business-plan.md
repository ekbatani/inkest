# Inkest Business Plan

## 1. Executive Summary

**Inkest** is a private, Markdown-first workspace for personal notes, lightweight project management, diagrams, images, and AI-assisted thinking.

The product is designed for users who want the simplicity and ownership of Markdown, the polish of a modern SaaS app, and the privacy of a self-hostable system. Unlike heavy all-in-one workspaces, Inkest focuses on a calm writing experience, clean project organization, and explicit AI tools that help users think, summarize, plan, and structure their notes without taking control away from them.

The initial product will be launched as a self-hosted web application using Next.js, Turso/libSQL, and local filesystem storage. The first commercial opportunity is a hosted cloud version with sync, backup, AI credits, and premium themes.

## 2. Product Name

**Name:** Inkest
**Domain:** inkest.app
**Tagline:** A calm Markdown workspace for notes, projects, and private thinking.

## 3. Vision

Inkest should become the cleanest place for independent thinkers, developers, designers, founders, writers, and technical professionals to keep their notes, plan projects, document ideas, and use AI safely.

The long-term vision is not to become a Notion clone. Inkest should become a personal operating system for structured thinking: fast, private, elegant, Markdown-native, and exportable.

## 4. Mission

Inkest helps people turn scattered notes into clear thinking, organized projects, and reusable knowledge without sacrificing privacy, simplicity, or ownership.

## 5. Problem

Many people now use multiple tools for notes, tasks, documents, diagrams, and AI. This creates several problems:

1. Notes are scattered across different apps.
2. Project context gets separated from project tasks.
3. AI tools are useful but often disconnected from personal knowledge.
4. Many popular tools are too complex for personal use.
5. Users do not always control their data.
6. Markdown users often lack a beautiful web-based interface.
7. RTL and multilingual writing support is often treated as secondary.
8. Self-hosted tools are often powerful but visually weak.

The core problem: users want a private, beautiful, structured workspace that feels simple but can grow with their thinking.

## 6. Solution

Inkest solves this by combining:

* Markdown notes
* Beautiful editor and preview
* Mermaid diagrams
* Local image attachments
* Lightweight project management
* Tags, folders, and search
* Explicit AI actions
* English-first interface
* RTL-ready note writing
* Self-hosted deployment
* Clean export and backup

Inkest should feel like a writing studio, not a corporate workspace.

## 8. Market Opportunity

The market is attractive because note-taking, personal knowledge management, and productivity software are already large categories. Users are familiar with tools like Notion, Obsidian, Standard Notes, Evernote, Anytype, Logseq, Bear, and Apple Notes.

However, the market still has space for a focused product because many existing tools force tradeoffs:

* Notion is polished but heavy and cloud-first.
* Obsidian is powerful and local-first but can feel plugin-heavy.
* Standard Notes is private but less project-oriented.
* Anytype is ambitious but can feel complex.
* Apple Notes is simple but not Markdown/project-native.
* Self-hosted tools often lack premium UI/UX.

Inkest should win by combining four qualities:

1. Beautiful UI/UX
2. Markdown-native data ownership
3. Self-hosted privacy
4. Useful AI actions

## 10. Unique Value Proposition

Inkest gives users a calm, private, beautiful place to write, organize, plan, and think in Markdown.

The key differentiators:

1. **Markdown-first**
   Notes remain portable and readable.

2. **UI/UX-first**
   The interface is not an afterthought. The product should feel premium from the first screen.

3. **Self-hosted MVP**
   Users can run it on their own server and keep images/files on their own filesystem.

4. **Project notes**
   Project management is built around notes, not a separate complicated system.

5. **Mermaid-native**
   Technical users can create flowcharts, diagrams, system designs, and plans directly inside notes.

6. **Explicit AI**
   AI is available when requested, not constantly running in the background.

7. **RTL-ready**
   English-first UI, but individual notes can support Persian, Arabic, Hebrew, and mixed-direction content.

## 17. Technology Plan

### MVP Stack

* Next.js App Router
* TypeScript
* Tailwind CSS
* shadcn/ui
* Drizzle ORM
* Turso/libSQL
* CodeMirror 6
* React Markdown
* Mermaid
* Auth.js
* Local filesystem storage
* OpenAI-compatible AI provider

### Why This Stack

This stack keeps the product simple, modern, and self-hostable. It also supports future migration to a hosted SaaS model without rewriting the entire system.

### Technical Principles

* Keep notes as Markdown text.
* Keep file storage separate from the database.
* Keep AI provider abstraction flexible.
* Keep all note access scoped by user.
* Keep export/import as a first-class feature.
* Keep the UI component system clean and reusable.

## 22. Main Risks

### Risk 1: Crowded Market

There are many note-taking apps.

Mitigation:

* Narrow positioning.
* Focus on Markdown, privacy, self-hosting, and UI quality.
* Do not compete directly with Notion on breadth.

### Risk 2: Too Many Features

The product can become complex quickly.

Mitigation:

* Keep Markdown as the core.
* Keep project management lightweight.
* Avoid collaboration until later.
* Avoid plugin systems in MVP.

### Risk 3: AI Privacy Concerns

Users may not want private notes sent to AI providers.

Mitigation:

* Make AI explicit.
* Show what will be sent.
* Support custom AI keys.
* Support local/self-hosted model providers later.
* Store AI logs transparently.

### Risk 4: Self-Hosted Users May Not Pay

Self-hosters often prefer free tools.

Mitigation:

* Monetize convenience, hosted sync, backup, AI, and support.
* Offer early supporter licenses.
* Offer managed deployment.

### Risk 5: UX Quality Takes Time

UI/UX is a core differentiator and cannot be rushed.

Mitigation:

* Build fewer features with higher polish.
* Treat onboarding, empty states, editor feel, and preview quality as core product work.
* Use a design system early.
